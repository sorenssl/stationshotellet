## The strategic question

You're doing direct-book today. The trade-off with OTAs is brutal but simple:

| | Direct-book (today) | Booking.com (15%) | Airbnb (~15%) | VRBO (8%) |
|---|---|---|---|---|
| Per-booking cost (on 990 kr/night, 7 nights) | **0 kr** | 1,039 kr lost | 1,039 kr lost | 554 kr lost |
| Per-month cost (on 14,000 kr/month) | **0 kr** | 2,100 kr lost | 2,100 kr lost | 1,120 kr lost |
| Guest discovery | You depend on SEO/word-of-mouth | Massive — Booking.com is #1 in Sweden | Massive — Airbnb dominates 30+ day stays | Smaller, more "vacation home" niche |
| Reviews | Manual collection (slow) | Automatic, drive trust | Automatic, drive trust | Automatic |
| Cancellation control | Yours (your tiers) | Booking.com policy applies | Airbnb policy applies | VRBO policy applies |
| Brand control | Full | Limited | Limited | Limited |
| Customer relationship | Direct | Booking.com mediates | Airbnb mediates | VRBO mediates |

**The right way to think about it**: each OTA booking pays for itself in the value of (a) the booking you wouldn't have gotten otherwise, plus (b) the review that helps your direct-book conversion later. If 30% of OTA guests would have found you anyway via Google, you're paying 5% effective commission for the other 70% — much better deal.

But that math only works AFTER your direct-book SEO is established. Right now, **stationshotellet.com isn't even indexed by Google** (verified 2026-05-09 — `site:stationshotellet.com` returned zero results). So an OTA listing during this gap has the highest ROI of its life: every booking is genuinely incremental.

## Per-platform deep dive

### 1. Booking.com — START HERE if you go OTA

**Audience**: #1 in Sweden for hotel/apartment search. Practically every Swedish business traveler has a Booking.com account. Your direct competitors (Centralt belägen villa i Öjebyn, Pite Havsbad, Skoogs City BnB, Piteå Stadshotell) are all on it.

**Cost**: ~15% commission (sometimes negotiable down to 12-13% for good performers; depends on country/property type). Charged on gross booking amount including any fees.

**No guest service fee**: Booking.com guests pay only the listed price. So if you list at 990 kr, they pay 990 kr, you receive ~840 kr.

**Pricing strategy on Booking.com**:
- List at **1,140 kr/night** (your 990 kr × 1.15 to absorb commission) — looks slightly under direct competitors (1,400-1,500), still your "lowest in Öjebyn" positioning relative to Booking.com's own market
- Or list at 1,290 kr (gives you an extra 100 kr margin to absorb the bigger discount Booking sometimes pushes hosts to give)

**Key warning**: Rate-parity clauses. Booking.com USED to require you offer them rates equal to or lower than direct-book. After EU rulings ~2015 they technically removed this in EEA, but they algorithmically deprioritize listings that are clearly cheaper elsewhere. **Don't be too obviously cheaper on direct-book** or your Booking.com listing will be buried.

**Setup time**: ~2-3 hours (KYC, photos, descriptions, calendar sync, payment setup with Swedish bank).

**Setup blockers for you**: Need exterior photo for Booking.com listing. Same blocker as Google Business Profile.

**Verdict**: **Start here Month 4 once direct-book is established.** 15% is the cost of fast volume.

### 2. Airbnb — ADD IN MONTH 6 IF NEEDED

**Audience**: Younger, more international, more vacation-oriented. Very strong for **30+ day stays** (their "monthly" segment is competitive). Healthcare contractors and remote workers heavily use this.

**Cost**: ~14-16% host-only fee model (changed in late 2025 — guests no longer see a separate service fee at checkout, hosts absorb it all). Or 3% host + ~14% guest service fee under split-fee model (legacy).

**Pricing strategy on Airbnb**:
- Same approach: list at 1,140-1,290 kr to absorb commission
- Airbnb is the BEST place to capture your monthly-stay healthcare audience — list 14,000 kr/month at ~16,500 kr

**Unique advantage**: Airbnb's "Monthly stays" filter and search ranking strongly favor longhost listings. Your 14,000 kr/month is positioned for this.

**Setup time**: ~1-2 hours (Airbnb is faster than Booking.com).

**Verdict**: Month 6 if you have surplus capacity and Booking.com isn't saturating.

---

### 3. VRBO — SKIP FOR NOW

**Audience**: US/UK-skewed, vacation-home / family-trip focused. Smaller in Sweden. Uses pay-per-booking now (5% + 3% processing = 8% effective).

**Cost**: 5% commission + 3% payment processing = ~8% effective. Cheaper than Booking/Airbnb.

**Why skip**: Tiny audience for Swedish 2-bedroom apartments. You'd spend 1-2 hours setting up and get maybe 1-2 bookings/year. Not worth the calendar-sync risk (double-booking with direct).

**Verdict**: Skip until you've maxed Booking.com + Airbnb.

### 4. HomeToGo / Casamundo / Holidu — DO NOTHING

These are **meta-aggregators**. They pull listings from Booking.com, Airbnb, VRBO, and others — they don't have their own host accounts. By listing on Booking.com you're automatically discoverable through HomeToGo/Casamundo. Zero additional action needed.

## Direct-book retention strategy (to make OTA listings less needed long-term)

The cheaper you can make it for guests to find you directly, the less you need OTAs:

1. **Google Business Profile** — finish it (you have an open item: exterior photo). GBP listings show up in "Map Pack" results which are FREE and dominate local search. **Highest-leverage action you can take right now.**
2. **Google Search Console** — verify ownership of stationshotellet.com, submit sitemap.xml. Get indexed.
3. **Bing Webmaster Tools** — same, takes 5 minutes. ~5% of Sweden uses Bing.
4. **First 10 reviews on Google + your site** — ask every direct guest. Reviews are the #1 conversion lever.
5. **Email list** — collect emails from inquirers, send a "stay 7 nights, get 1 free" offer 3 months in.

If you do these 5 things, your OTA dependence in Year 2 is near-zero.

## What NOT to do (mistakes I've seen others make)

❌ **List on every OTA at once**. Calendar-sync hell. You'll double-book once and lose Doushka's Saturday to manually canceling someone.

❌ **Set OTA prices same as direct-book**. Booking.com algorithm may bury you for "rate parity" issues. Always price 10-15% higher on OTAs to absorb commission.

❌ **Pay for promoted listings on Booking.com** until you have 5+ reviews. Money wasted — guests filter by review count.

❌ **Sign up for OTA "Genius" / "Superhost" programs immediately**. Wait until you have organic 5-star reviews. Programs typically require 5-10% extra discount on top of commission.

❌ **Forget to set minimum stay rules per platform**. On Booking.com, set 2-night minimum to avoid 1-night turnovers eating your operating margin.

## Math: when does an OTA pay for itself?

At your nightly rate of 990 kr direct / 1,140 kr OTA:

- Direct-book: 990 kr × 365 nights = 361,350 kr/year if 100% occupancy
- 50% occupancy direct only: 180,675 kr
- 50% occupancy split (30% direct, 20% Booking): (135 nights × 990) + (90 nights × 1,140 × 0.85) = **133,650 + 87,210 = 220,860 kr** vs 180,675 direct-only

**OTA listing pays off if it brings ≥10 incremental nights/year that you wouldn't have gotten direct.** That's almost certainly true given your indexing situation today.

## Honest caveats

- **Bookings.com commission**: I've seen 15% as the commonly-quoted rate; it's negotiable down to 12% for hosts that perform well, and may start at 17% for properties Booking.com considers harder to fill. Will know for certain at signup.
- **Airbnb late-2025 fee change**: I cited the host-only fee model (~14-16%). Some Swedish hosts may still be on legacy split-fee. Verify in your host dashboard at signup.
- **Cancellation policy mismatch**: Your tiered policy (3-day flexible / 7-day moderate / 1-month deposit) is finer-grained than what most OTAs natively support. You'll have to map onto their preset options:
  - Booking.com: closest = "Flexible" or "Moderate"
  - Airbnb: closest = "Flexible" or "Moderate"
  - **Best practical mapping**: use "Flexible" on the OTA platform (free cancel up to 5 days before) — slightly more generous than your direct policy. Then guests who book direct get the slightly tighter policy as a benefit.
